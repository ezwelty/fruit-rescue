Coordinator Command Center

Thank you for stepping up to coordinate a harvest!

Once you've been assigned to a harvest, you’ll receive an email with the harvest details (resident's name, address, and contact info, tree details, etc).

  1. Pick up equipment and blank paperwork from our storage hub on the day of your harvest.
  2. Check in with the residents when you arrive. Ask them for containers for their share (if desired) and their compost bin for rotten fruit (we pick up rotten fruit so that it doesn't attract wildlife).
  3. Put out the sign so volunteers can find the harvest.
  4. Welcome volunteers and read them the harvest briefing. Have them sign in and sign a release (if they haven't already registered online). We strongly encourage you to bring a mobile device so that people can sign the release online at fruitrescue.org/release.
  5. Manage the harvest by putting out new boxes when they are full, answering questions, etc.
  6. Help weigh the fruit, recording weights in the harvest log. Divide the fruit for residents, harvesters, and donation.
  7. Transport fruit and equipment back to the storage hub by the end of the day.

Our storage space is located at Hope Church (4705 Baseline Rd), in the northernmost white building on the property. This is where donated fruit and harvest equipment are picked up and dropped off. You'll receive the access code with the harvest details.

Map directions

Pickup instructions (Instructions are also posted at the storage hub)

Pick up all of these items on the day of your harvest:

  • Picking poles
  • Scale
  • Easel sign (with markers - should be in a bag attached to the sign)
  • Paperwork and clipboard (in the "blank forms" envelope in the black metal file sorter):
    • Harvest briefing (1 sheet)
    • Sign in sheet / Harvest log (1 sheet, front and back)
    • Several releases
    • Several flyers (in case anyone asks what you’re up to)
Make sure to lock up when you leave.

Dropoff instructions (Instructions are also posted at the storage hub)

Please return all equipment, paperwork, and fruit after your harvest:

  • Place the completed paperwork in the “completed forms” envelope.
  • If you have boxes with fruit for wildlife, label them with wildlife signs (in the file sorter).
Make sure to lock up when you leave.

If you have a question or need assistance, email us at info@fruitrescue.org.
