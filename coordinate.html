Coordinator Command Center

Thank you for stepping up to coordinate a harvest!

If you are coordinating a harvest for someone else, first choose which trees to harvest.

whose residents request assistance – map list (high priority locations with engaged residents).

available for open harvest – map list (low priority locations with disengaged residents).

Confirm your plans with the resident (it not you), then email us (ideally, with two+ weeks notice). Each week, we announce upcoming harvests to our volunteers.

  • If you can deliver the donated fruit to our storage hub, schedule the harvest for whenever is most convenient – bearing in mind that fruit must be stored in a bear-proof location until it is delivered.
  • If you need us to deliver the donated fruit, schedule the harvest on a Sunday and plan to be finished by 3pm. Volunteers will pick up the fruit between 3-5pm.

Plan to visit only one site per harvest, since most volunteers leave after harvesting the first site. If sites are close together, feel free to schedule multiple harvests on the same day – keeping in mind the # and size of trees at each site and the distance between sites.

:) Encourage them to register at fruitrescue.org/join so they receive email reminders and sign the release form online.

Our storage space is located at Hope Church (4705 Baseline Rd), in the northernmost, white building on the property. This is where donated fruit is delivered, and where any needed harvest equipment is picked up and dropped off. Email us for the access code.

Before the harvest, prepare the paperwork and harvesting equipment.

  • Harvest briefing – to read to participants at the start of the event.
  • Sign in sheet & pen – so we know who participated.
  • Release agreements & pens – for participants who haven't yet registered. They can also complete the form online at fruitrescue.org/release.
  • Harvest log & pen – to record the weight of the fruit harvested.

    Provided by you / the resident:
  • Ladders – (if necessary and available).
  • Containers / tarps – for the ⅓ (or less) going to you / the resident (if desired).

    Available for pick up at our storage hub (in limited supply):
  • Fruit picking poles
  • Bathroom scales – to weigh the fruit.
  • Containers – for the ⅓ (or more) going to charity.

During the harvest, welcome and direct participants.

  • Read them the Harvest briefing.
  • Show them the sign in sheet and release forms, the fruit tree(s) to harvest, the scales and harvest log. Show them where to put fruit for you (if desired) and fruit for charity.
  • Ensure they are being safe and respectful of the property and the trees, reminding them of the rules if necessary.
  • If you will be moving on to another site, let them know as departure time approaches and announce the next address.

After the harvest, pack up the donated fruit, borrowed equipment, and completed paperwork.

  • If you are delivering to the storage hub, do so right away or store the donated fruit in a bear-proof location.
  • If you arranged for a pickup, please put everything out in a tidy pile by the curb.

Congrats on being awesome, we couldn’t do this without you!
